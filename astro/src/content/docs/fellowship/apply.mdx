## Apply to the Regenerative Engineering Fellowship

### Application Requirements

**To apply, you should have:**

- **Basic programming skills** — Comfortable with at least one programming language
- **Interest in regenerative principles** — Genuine curiosity about ethical software
- **Willingness to learn** — Open to new ideas and ways of thinking
- **Communication skills** — Can work well in teams and explain your thinking

**You don't need:**

- DevOps experience
- Lean certification
- Strong systems thinking background
- Prior Debian/Open Source knowledge

**We teach you everything else.**

### Application Process

**Stage 1: Online Application** (30 minutes)

Submit:

- Resume or CV
- Cover letter (1–2 pages) answering:
  - What draws you to regenerative software engineering?
  - Describe a time you learned something difficult
  - What does ethical technology mean to you?
- Portfolio or code samples (GitHub, personal projects, etc.)

**Stage 2: Technical Screening** (45 minutes, video call)

We discuss:

- Your technical background and learning style
- Participation in previous projects
- Problem-solving approach
- Why you're interested in this specific opportunity

**Stage 3: Cultural Fit Interview** (30 minutes, in-person or video)

We explore:

- Your values and how they align with ours
- How you handle disagreement or uncertainty
- Your questions about the role and organization

**Stage 4: Final Decision**

- Feedback from all interviewers
- Offer (or constructive feedback if not selected)

**Timeline:** Full process typically takes 3–4 weeks.

### Evaluation Criteria

We're looking for people who will thrive in our environment.

**Technical capability** (40%)

- Do you have foundational programming skills? Are you capable of growth?
- Problem-solving approach? Can you debug systematically?

**Learning mindset** (30%)

- Are you genuinely curious? Will you seek to understand, not just execute?
- Do you embrace feedback? Can you learn from mistakes?

**Regenerative values** (20%)

- Do you care about ethical software? Is it genuine, not performative?
- Can you think systemically? Do you consider ripple effects?

**Communication & collaboration** (10%)

- Can you articulate your thinking clearly?
- Do you listen well and work well with others?

### Logistics

**Program dates:** January–December annually (also rolling enrollment possible)

**Location:** Remote-first, but some team time expected (we're in Copenhagen)

**Time commitment:** 37.5 hours/week (defined working hours)

**Compensation:** 14,000–18,000 DKK/month + benefits

**Benefits:**

- Health insurance
- Equipment and home office setup
- Learning budget (€500)
- Office access when in Copenhagen
- Conference attendance support

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I have no professional experience. Can I apply?**

A: Absolutely. We're looking for people committed to learning, not just people who already know everything.

**Q: Do I need to relocate to Copenhagen?**

A: No. We're remote-first. You might visit for team gatherings (1–2x/year), but daily work is wherever you are.

**Q: What if I'm not sure it's the right fit?**

A: The interview process is two-way. Ask us anything. We want mutual fit.

**Q: What happens after the fellowship?**

A: You'll be evaluated for advancing to Associate level (L2). If you're not quite ready, we discuss options. If we don't have a permanent role available, we're proud of you and provide strong references.

**Q: How is this different from traditional internships?**

A: You're valued as a real team member, not disposable labor. You get mentorship, real projects, governance participation, and full compensation from day 1.

**Q: Can I apply if I'm not in the EU?**

A: We're exploring this. Our base operations are in Denmark, which has EU working rights frameworks. Contact us to discuss options.
