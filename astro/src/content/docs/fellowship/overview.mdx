## The Regenerative Engineering Fellowship

The **Regenerative Engineering Fellowship** is a structured learning program for emerging engineers and students.

It's not a traditional internship focused on "cheap labor." It's a genuine apprenticeship where fellows learn regenerative principles through real work in our Community of Practice.

### What is the Fellowship?

The Fellowship is a **12-month program** (renewable annually) where:

- **You learn** regenerative software engineering principles, not just programming
- **You work** on real client projects alongside experienced practitioners
- **You participate** in the Community of Practice and organizational governance
- **You contribute** to open-source projects that advance cultural heritage
- **You earn** a meaningful stipend while learning

### Who Should Apply

**We're looking for:**

- **Emerging software engineers** (0–2 years experience)
- **Computer science students** (senior year or recent graduates)
- **Career-transitioners** (changing into software development)
- **People committed to regenerative principles** (not just code)

**No prior DevOps or Lean experience required** — we'll teach you.

**We value:**

- Genuine curiosity about systems thinking
- Willingness to learn (not "I know what I want to do")
- Ethics and integrity above technical cleverness
- Collaboration and communication skills
- Commitment to growth beyond personal advancement

### Program Structure

**Timeline:**

- **Months 1–3** — Onboarding, foundational learning, observation
- **Months 4–9** — Growing independence, taking on responsibility
- **Months 10–12** — Leadership, mentoring peers, advanced projects

**Time Commitment:**

- 37.5 hours/week
- Flexible location (can be remote)
- No unpaid overtime (we practice what we preach)
- Dedicated mentorship (1–2 hours/week with senior colleague)

**Financial:**

- **Stipend:** 14,000–18,000 DKK/month (entry-level salary range)
- **Benefits:** Health insurance, equipment, learning budget
- **No unpaid trial period** — You're compensated from day 1

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of the Fellowship, you will understand:

- **Regenerative principles** — What does ethical software really mean?
- **Systems thinking** — How do complex systems evolve and maintain health?
- **Lean practices** — How to eliminate waste and optimize for sustainability
- **Participatory design** — How to center user needs in development
- **Green algorithms** — How to write energy-efficient code
- **DevX** — How to build sustainable, healthy engineering cultures
- **Open source** — How to contribute to the global commons
- **Democracy** — How to participate in organization governance

### Advancing From Fellowship

**After your Fellowship:**

- **Pathway to Associate (L2)** — If you demonstrate mastery of L1 criteria
- **Opportunity for permanent role** — If we have capacity and it's good fit
- **Alumni network** — Stay connected to the community
- **Strong reference** — Regardless of next step

**Our success metric:** You become an ethical, skilled, regenerative engineer — whether with us or elsewhere.

### The Triad Alliance

The Fellowship is a three-way collaboration:

| Partner                  | Role                                                    |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Academic Institution** | Provides theoretical grounding, credits if applicable   |
| **Client-Member**        | Provides real-world project, mentoring, workspace       |
| **Lean C.R.O.W.D.**      | Provides structure, community, governance participation |

You're not isolated in a training program. You're embedded in our actual work.
