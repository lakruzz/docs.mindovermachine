## Purpose

The representative body is the foundation's advisory consultation body with professional and ethical roots in the ecosystem.

The body must strengthen the quality of the board's decision-making basis and ensure continuous dialogue with the environments in which the foundation operates.

## Authorization

The framework is based on the foundation's articles of association, including the provisions on the advisory role of the board of representatives, the annual meeting and the right to appoint up to two board positions.

In the event of a conflict between this document and the articles of association, the articles of association take precedence.

## Role and mandate

The representative body is advisory and does not have management responsibility for the fund.

The board continues to have full responsibility for decisions, supervision and priorities.

The representative body can make recommendations on strategy, ethics, direction of distribution and significant governance issues.

The representative body can appoint up to two members to the board of directors within the framework of the articles of association.

## Establishment and composition

The board appoints the board of representatives in cooperation with the executive board.

The composition should reflect the foundation's ecosystem, for example contributors, practitioners, thinkers and staff.

The board determines the size, appointment period and criteria for composition with a focus on professionalism, diversity, independence and legitimacy.

The board publishes the overall principles for composition and appointment.

## Meetings and working style

The representative body meets at least once a year at the annual meeting.

Meetings can be held physically or digitally.

The board can call for extra consultation meetings for important strategic or structural issues.

The person in charge of the board or an appointed representative participates as a starting point in the annual meeting to ensure consistency between consultation and decision.

The management supports meeting preparation, distribution of material and minutes.

## Mandatory hearings

The representative body is consulted before significant changes to the articles of association.

The representative body is consulted in case of significant changes in the fund's strategic direction or governance structure.

The representative body is consulted before the board adopts significant, long-term changes in principles for distribution direction or ecosystem anchoring.

The board can also choose to carry out voluntary hearings in other cases.

## Right of appointment to the board

When the board of representatives exercises its right of appointment, the process must be structured, documented and transparent.

Candidates are assessed against the board's overall competence needs, ability and statutory requirements.

The representative body's recommendation is recorded and included in the board's overall documentation of the appointment process.

## Ability, confidentiality and documentation

Members of the board of representatives must disclose matters that may create conflicts of interest in specific cases.

Confidential information is treated with the same care as in the foundation's other governance work.

A decision report or consultation note is prepared after each meeting with a clear indication of recommendations and any points of disagreement.

## Relationship to year wheel

The representative body's annual meeting is planned as part of the foundation's annual cycle.

Results from hearings and the annual meeting are processed by the board at the next ordinary meeting and translated into concrete follow-up points.

## Annual review

The board evaluates at least once a year whether the board of representatives functions according to its purpose and provides sufficient value in the decision-making process.

This document is updated as needed and at least once a year.
