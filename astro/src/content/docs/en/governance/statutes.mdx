## About the document

The statutes are the foundation's constitution. They determine the fund's purpose, management structure and legal framework.

This page publishes the applicable statute text so that it is easily accessible to the public.

## Name, nickname and place of residence

**§ 1.**

Paragraph 1. The name of the foundation is "The Foundation for Regenerative Software".

Paragraph 2. The foundation's name is:

- "Mind over Machine"
- "DevOpsDays Denmark"

Paragraph 3. The foundation's registered office is Copenhagen Municipality.

## Founder

**§ 2.**

Paragraph 1. The foundation was established by the following in association:

- The association DevOpsDays Copenhagen (CVR: 39299127)
- KruseControl (CVR: 25317459)

Paragraph 2. The foundation takes place by depositing the share capital and as a continuation of the visions of the founding units.

## Purpose

**§ 3.**

Paragraph 1. The foundation's overall purpose is to search for, explore and disseminate a model for how software and software development can contribute to a regenerative paradigm that creates positive value for people, society and the world.

Paragraph 2. The foundation assumes that the term "regenerative" in the context of software and software development implies that both product and process are regenerative, healing or rebuilding.

Paragraph 3. The foundation operates within a socio-technological discourse and agenda with a focus on the ethical and philosophical meeting between man and machine as well as on quality in both software products and processes.

Paragraph 4. The purpose is sought to be realized through three areas of action:

- Think tank: Research, analysis and dissemination of long-term systems thinking and regenerative principles within disciplines related to or derived from software and software development.
- Laboratory: Development of software, tools and methods that demonstrate regenerative principles in practice.
- Community of practice: Facilitation of a community of practice (Community of Practice) for practitioners, researchers, students and decision-makers with a view to promoting regenerative software, software development and technology understanding.

Paragraph 5. The foundation is a business operator and can run a business that has a direct or indirect connection with the purpose.

## The fund's capital and special rights

**§ 4.**

Paragraph 1. The foundation's share capital amounts to DKK 300,000. The capital is paid in cash by the founders in association.

Paragraph 2. The founders or other special rights or benefits have not been granted.

## The Board of Directors

**§ 5.**

Paragraph 1. The foundation is managed by a board of 3-5 members.

Paragraph 2. The board members are appointed for a period of 2 years. Reelection may take place.

Paragraph 3. Appointment of the board takes place in the following way:

- Up to 2 members can be appointed by the Representative Council, cf. § 6 subsection 3.
- The other members are appointed by the sitting board (self-replenishment) on the basis of professional, ethical and otherwise relevant characteristics.

Paragraph 4. The board constitutes itself with a chairman and a deputy chairman.

## The representative office

**§ 6.**

Paragraph 1. To ensure the foundation's professional and ethical anchoring, the board, in collaboration with the director, appoints a board of representatives consisting of representatives from the foundation's ecosystem, for example contributors, practitioners, thinkers or employees.

Paragraph 2. The representative body meets at least once a year at the annual meeting.

Paragraph 3. The representative office functions as an advisory body for the foundation's strategic direction and can appoint up to 2 members to the board in accordance with § 5, subsection 3.

Paragraph 4. The annual meeting and other activities in the board of representatives can be conducted digitally without the need for physical attendance.

## The board's work and daily management

**§ 7.**

Paragraph 1. The board has the strategic management of the fund. Decisions are made by simple majority vote, unless otherwise follows from section 11, subsection 1. In the event of a tie, the vote of the first person is decisive.

Paragraph 2. The board appoints a director to handle the day-to-day operations of the foundation's units (Think Tank, Laboratory and Community of Practice). As a rule, the director participates ex officio, without the right to vote, in all meetings of the board, unless the board decides otherwise in the individual case.

Paragraph 3. The board's meetings can be held using electronic media (digital meetings) if the person in charge deems it appropriate and it is compatible with the performance of the board's tasks.

Paragraph 4. The board, in cooperation with the director, determines the detailed framework, conditions and intentions for the work in the foundation's areas of action (§ 3) in internal instructions or rules of procedure.

## Accounting and auditing

**§ 8.**

Paragraph 1. The foundation's financial year follows the calendar year.

Paragraph 2. The annual accounts must be audited by a state authorized or registered auditor.

## Use of profits and distribution

**§ 9.**

Paragraph 1. The fund's surplus must, after necessary appropriations for consolidation of the fund, be used to promote the fund's purpose, cf. § 3.

Paragraph 2. The board decides on the distribution of funds for projects, research or initiatives that promote a deeper understanding of the regenerative paradigm.

## Drawing rule

**§ 10.**

Paragraph 1. The fund is subscribed by the entire board of directors, by the sponsor in association with another board member, or by the sponsor in association with a director.

## Amendments to articles of association and dissolution

**§ 11.**

Paragraph 1. Amendments to the articles of association require unanimity in the board and must be approved by the fund authority. The representative body must be consulted before significant changes.

Paragraph 2. Upon dissolution of the foundation, its remaining assets must be used for non-profit purposes within socio-technological research or regenerative technology as determined by the board of directors and with the approval of the foundation authority.
