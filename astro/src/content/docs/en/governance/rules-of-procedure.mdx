---
title: Rules of procedure for the board
description: Rules of procedure for the Foundation for Regenerative Software
---

For commercial foundations, it is a legal requirement that the board draw up rules of procedure.

These rules of procedure describe how the board of the Foundation for Regenerative Software carries out its work in practice.

## Purpose and authority

These rules of procedure determine the detailed rules for the board's work in the Foundation for Regenerative Software in accordance with Section 50 of the Business Foundations Act.
The rules of procedure must be interpreted in accordance with the foundation's articles of association. In the event of a conflict, the articles of association take precedence.
The rules of procedure are reviewed at least once a year and updated as necessary.

## Establishment and division of labor

The board constitutes itself with a chairman and deputy chairman in accordance with the articles of association.
The person in charge organizes, convenes and leads the board meetings.
The deputy person takes the place of the person in case of default.
The board determines a clear division of labor between the board, the representative, any committees and the executive board.
In a separate document, the board determines the framework for the establishment, operation, consultation and right of appointment of the board of representatives, cf. [Board of Representatives](/en/governance/representative-council/).
If the board assigns a board member special tasks in addition to the board position, this must be decided by the board and recorded.

## Meeting frequency, convening and decision-making basis

The board holds the necessary meetings and at least four ordinary meetings annually.
The person in charge prepares an annual plan for the board's work, including a plan for meetings and fixed topics.
The Year Wheel is maintained in the separate document [Year Wheel](/en/governance/annual-cycle/).
The notice is sent with the agenda and relevant material in a reasonable time before the meeting and, as a rule, no later than five working days before the meeting.
The board can hold physical, digital, written and electronic meetings when it is compatible with the board's tasks.
The board has decision-making capacity when more than half of the members participate.
A board member can demand that a topic be included on the agenda.
Decisions are made according to the rules of the articles of association.
An extraordinary board meeting is held when a board member, the executive board or the auditor requests this.
The agenda must contain, as a minimum, formalities, decision points, reporting from the executive board and follow-up on outstanding matters.

## Protocol, minutes and documentation

Decision minutes are kept for all board meetings.
At a minimum, the minutes must state the time, participants, qualifications, decisions and any dissenting opinions.
Minutes are approved by the board and kept securely.
Important instructions, policies and decisions are systematically journalised.

## Confidentiality, competence and conflicts of interest

Board members, management and any observers are subject to a duty of confidentiality regarding confidential matters.
A member may not participate in the processing of cases in which the member has significant personal or financial interests.
Competency is clarified at the start of the meeting and in connection with specific cases, and the result is recorded.
An incapacitated member is not included in the processing of the case in question.

## Management, reporting and supervision

The board supervises the executive board's day-to-day management.
The management reports to the board in a timely, accurate and sufficient manner on operations, finances, risks and significant dispositions.
The board determines a formalized reporting framework, including minimum requirements for periodic management reporting.
The board evaluates the work of the executive board and the cooperation between the board and the executive board at least once a year.

## Accounting, internal control and auditing

The board ensures that bookkeeping and financial statements are carried out in accordance with legislation and good practice.
The board receives ongoing financial reporting and carries out the necessary accounting controls.
The board ensures that the auditor has the necessary basis for the audit at all times.
Audit minutes are submitted to the board without undue delay and signed by all board members after consideration.
At each ordinary board meeting, any new additions to the audit protocol are reviewed.

## Strategy, asset management, distribution policy and communication

At least once a year, the board takes a position on the fund's overall strategy based on the articles of association.
The board regularly takes a position on whether the fund's capital management corresponds to the fund's purpose and needs in the short and long term.
The board determines and revises principles for distribution policy as necessary.
The board adopts general principles for external communication, which ensure relevant openness towards stakeholders.
The spokesperson speaks on behalf of the board, unless the board decides otherwise in the specific case or generally delegates to the executive board.
The board identifies the fund's most significant strategic, operational and accounting risks at least once a year.

## Self-evaluation

At least once a year, the board carries out an evaluation of the board's work, the representative's management of the board's work and the contributions of the individual members.
The form of the evaluation is determined by the board, taking into account the fund's size and complexity.
Results and any follow-up points are recorded.
At least once a year, the board conducts an ethical review of good fund management based on the Committee's form for reporting on good fund management.

## Selection and nomination of board candidates

The board approves a structured, thorough and transparent process for the selection and nomination of candidates for the board.
The process must take into account the rules of the articles of association on the right of appointment.
The process and the criteria used are recorded.

## Substitutes and other matters

The foundation does not have deputies, unless the board decides to propose an amendment to the articles of association.
The board decides at appropriate intervals whether the arrangement is still appropriate.

## Entry into force

These rules of procedure come into force upon adoption by the board.
The rules of procedure are adopted by the board and entered in the board's minutes with a date.
