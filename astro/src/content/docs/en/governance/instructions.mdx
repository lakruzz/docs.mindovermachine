## Purpose

This document brings together the instructions that govern the foundation's day-to-day operations.

The ambition is simple: The public must be able to see how we actually work, and internally we must have a clear, usable framework for decisions, responsibility and quality.

The instructions are therefore written in readable language. They are operational, but they must also be read as an open narrative about how the fund is managed.

## The Board of Directors

The board has strategic responsibility for the fund's direction, capital and integrity. This means that the board's work is not only about control, but about keeping the course firmly on the purpose, even when everyday life pulls in other directions.

The board works according to an annual cycle with fixed themes: strategy, direction of distribution, capital management, risks, evaluation and public reporting. The meetings are organized so that decisions can be made on an informed basis, and so that follow-up does not become a formality, but a real part of management.

The specific rhythm and the fixed deliveries are described in [Annual Wheel](/en/governance/annual-cycle/).

The board also maintains separate instructions for the board of representatives as an advisory consultation body, including processes for consultation and right of appointment, cf. [Representatives] (/en/governance/representative-council/).

When the board appoints new members, it is based on an overall assessment of competences, continuity, renewal and diversity. Independence is assessed and documented annually, so that it is transparent which ties may exist and how they are handled.

The board annually publishes relevant information on composition, remuneration and key management relationships in accordance with applicable regulations and good fund management.

## The management

The management manages the day-to-day operations within the framework established by the articles of association, rules of procedure and the board of directors. The role is both executive and committed to clear reporting.

The board is responsible for ensuring that the fund's operations, finances, quality and internal controls are sound. Significant or extraordinary dispositions are submitted to the board, and in urgent situations a fixed practice is followed to inform immediately and prior to processing.

The management regularly reports on the status, risks, significant dispositions and expectations for the coming period. The purpose of the reporting is not only to inform, but to make the board's supervision effective.

## Employees

Employees work within a clear framework of professional quality, ethical responsibility and documented practice. This applies both in development work, collaboration with stakeholders and internal knowledge sharing.

We expect employees to act loyally in relation to the foundation's purpose, protect confidential information and draw attention to conditions that may challenge quality, compliance with the law or ethical integrity.

The work is organized so that learning, improvement and responsibility go hand in hand. This means, among other things, that significant decisions are documented, that errors are used constructively for improvement, and that roles and expectations are clear in the collaboration between employees, management and the board.

## Future vision: Organization by growth

When the foundation hires its first employee, we will gradually implement principles for transparent and sustainable management. This section describes the values ​​and practices we want to establish when creating an organizational culture.

Employees must have both professional quality and a financial interest in the foundation's success. We will work with:

- **Transparent competence and salary structure:** Clear level and role categories with known salary information, based on market conditions and internal equality.
- **Mentor scheme:** Each employee gets an experienced colleague as a mentor, inspired by the principles of _senpai-kohai_, to ensure professional development and a clear path to advancement.
- **Financial share:** When the fund's finances permit, employees will receive a share in annual profits through a bonus scheme that ensures they take ownership of the fund's results.
- **Annual evaluation:** Meeting between employee, mentor and management about development, well-being and any changes in role or level.

These principles will be formalized in internal HR documents and evaluated annually as part of the foundation's ethical review.

## Annual update

The instructions are reviewed at least once a year in connection with the foundation's ethical assessment and annual report.

In the event of changes, the document is updated with clear justification, so that both internal and external stakeholders can follow developments in the fund's management.
