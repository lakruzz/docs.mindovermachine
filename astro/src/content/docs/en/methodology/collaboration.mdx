## Purpose

This page describes how companies can collaborate with the foundation through membership, contributions and projects.

The aim is to create a transparent model where partners can support the foundation's long-term work and at the same time gain concrete value in the ongoing dialogue and development.

## What membership is

Membership is a business model for cooperation with the foundation.

Membership gives access to a structured collaborative process, professional sparring, joint learning and prioritized access to the laboratory's capacity.

Membership also finances the common infrastructure around method development, knowledge sharing and the Community of Practice.

## What membership is not

Membership does not give ownership of the fund.

Membership does not give the right to vote in the foundation's management or the right to instruct the board.

Membership does not automatically give you a seat on the board of representatives.

Governance rights follow statutes and governance documents, not commercial agreements.

## Forms of cooperation

Cooperation can take place in three tracks.

1. Membership with ongoing quota and access to joint activities.
2. Project agreements with concrete delivery, scope and time frame.
3. Combined courses where membership and projects support each other.

Projects are always priced separately. Membership can provide better access, faster start-up and more continuous learning, but does not replace a concrete project agreement.

## Indicative membership levels

The levels here are a first draft. They are inspired by price models in comparable non-profit networks, professional communities and foundations with partnership models in the Nordics.

All amounts are stated per year excl. VAT

### Base

DKK 12,000

Suitable for smaller organizations, startups and government entities with limited budgets.

Contents:

- Access to open member sessions and selected professional formats.
- Quarterly strategic sparring, up to 2 hours per quarter.
- Early insight into method development and upcoming initiatives.

### Partner

DKK 36,000

Suitable for organizations with active development needs and a desire for closer cooperation.

Contents:

- Everything from Basic.
- Monthly sparring, up to 2 hours per month.
- Priority access to the laboratory's capacity planning.
- Possibility of co-creation courses and small pilot initiatives.

### Strategic partner

DKK 96,000

Suitable for organizations with multi-year cooperation and a high degree of joint ambition.

Contents:

- Everything from Partner.
- Quarterly management dialogue with the executive board.
- Participation in annual learning collection about method and effect.
- Possibility of reserved capacity window by separate agreement.

## Projects and discount principle

Project work is settled according to a separate agreement.

As a starting point, membership can trigger a reduction in project start-up costs or access to better capacity conditions, but not an automatic fixed discount on all deliveries.

The specific model is determined by the executive board within the board's general principles.

## Roles and responsibilities

The board determines the overall principles for ethics, competence, transparency and risk frameworks in the cooperation model.

The management determines the specific commercial model, price points, contract terms and capacity allocation.

The executive board reports at least once a year to the board on the membership model, effects, risks and any adjustments.

## Annual evaluation and public transparency

The model is evaluated at least once a year as part of the foundation's annual review.

The foundation publishes general principles for membership and cooperation, while specific contract terms and business-sensitive information are handled confidentially.

Prices and content can be adjusted if necessary, so that the model remains fair, sustainable and in line with the fund's purpose.
