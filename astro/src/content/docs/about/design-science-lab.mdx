## The Design Science Laboratory

The Lean C.R.O.W.D. is fundamentally a **Design Science Laboratory** — a unique entity that merges two complementary forces:

### The Think-Tank: Philosophy Meets Research

The think-tank provides the **"What" and "Why"**:

- **What should regenerative software engineering actually look like?**
- **Why do current models fail people and the planet?**
- **How do socio-technical systems evolve?**
- **What role can software play in ecological restoration?**

The think-tank conducts rigorous research into:

- Digital sustainability and energy consumption of software
- Socio-technical transitions and organizational transformation
- The "downward slope" of social complexity
- Regenerative computing frameworks and principles
- Developer wellbeing and human flourishing

Our research is embedded in practice, not isolated in academia. Every consulting project is a case study. Every client challenge is an opportunity to test and refine our theories.

### The Consultancy: Mechanical Application

The consultancy provides the **"How"**:

- **How do we actually build software differently?**
- **How do we integrate regenerative principles into real projects?**
- **How do we help organizations transition from extractive to regenerative models?**

The consultancy applies proven practices at scale:

- **Lean** principles to eliminate waste
- **DevOps** and **Continuous Delivery** for sustainable operations
- **Participatory Design** to center user needs
- **Toyota Way** systems thinking to optimize for human flourishing
- **Green Algorithms** to minimize digital waste

### The Laboratory

Where they merge, we create **a living laboratory** where theory and practice inform each other:

**Philosophy to be effective must be mechanically applied.** — R. Buckminster Fuller

We don't just theorize about a better world of software. We **build it**, observe it, learn from it, and share those learnings.

This creates a virtuous cycle:

1. The think-tank identifies systemic problems (e.g., developer burnout)
2. The consultancy tests solutions with real clients
3. We measure the impact and refine the approach
4. We publish our findings back to the community
5. The think-tank incorporates new learnings into the framework

### Why Both Parts Are Essential

**The think-tank alone** would be important but ineffective — producing interesting but untested theories.

**The consultancy alone** would be pragmatic but shortsighted — delivering projects without contributing to systemic change.

**Together**, they create something rare: an organization that is both intellectually rigorous and practically effective. We are credible theorists because we live our theories. We are effective practitioners because we have a coherent, research-backed framework.

### Our Community of Practice

The laboratory isn't just internal. It includes:

- **Client-members** who share in our mission and contribute to our research through their projects
- **Fellows and interns** who learn regenerative practices while contributing to real work
- **Open source contributors** who amplify our impact globally
- **Academic partners** who collaborate on socio-technical research
- **Board advisors** who provide strategic guidance

Everyone in this ecosystem participates in the learning. Everyone contributes to advancing regenerative software engineering.

### How We Operate

**Research Cycles** (Quarterly)

- Identify emerging challenges from consulting work
- Formulate research questions and hypotheses
- Design experiments or interventions
- Measure outcomes rigorously

**Knowledge Sharing** (Continuous)

- Publish field guides on digital sustainability
- Share reflective practice journals from our team
- Contribute tools and code to open source
- Present at conferences and community events

**Capability Development** (Ongoing)

- Advanced training for staff on new regenerative frameworks
- Mentorship from senior practitioners (Levels 4-6)
- Cross-project learning sessions
- Peer reviews and ethical audits

**Impact Measurement** (Annual)

- Lifecycle analysis of our consultancy's environmental impact
- Social impact assessment for client projects
- Financial analysis of the non-profit model
- Community feedback and continuous improvement

### The Phoenix: Praqma 2.0

This laboratory evolved from **Praqma**, a pioneering DevOps consultancy that spent two decades proving that lean software engineering wasn't just a theory — it could transform real organizations.

The merger with the **think-tank** represents an evolution: we're not just optimizing existing systems, we're questioning the systems themselves.

The tag-line remains true: **"We want to make the world a better place — at least the world of software."**

But now we have a vehicle to do it: a Design Science Laboratory that combines the best of both theory and practice.
