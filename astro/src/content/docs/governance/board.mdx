## The Professional Board

The **Professional Board** is a strategic advisory body elected by the General Assembly. It provides fiduciary oversight, ethical guidance, and strategic planning support to the C-level leadership.

The Board does not make day-to-day operational decisions. Instead, it ensures the organization stays true to its mission, maintains financial health, and serves all stakeholders fairly.

### Composition

The Board typically consists of **5 members**, serving **2-year terms**.

**Board Composition:**

| Seat     | Background                               | Voting Rights         |
| -------- | ---------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| 1-2      | Employees elected by employees           | Yes                   |
| 1-2      | Client-members elected by members        | Yes                   |
| 1        | External advisor with relevant expertise | Yes                   |
| Optional | Fund-contributor representative          | Yes if primary funder |

**Desired expertise on the Board:**

- Non-profit governance and best practices
- Regenerative business models
- Financial management and auditing
- Organizational culture and change management
- Socio-technical systems thinking
- Software engineering leadership

### Key Responsibilities

#### 1. **Fiduciary Oversight**

The Board ensures the organization manages financial resources responsibly.

| Oversight Area          | Details                                                       |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Annual Budget**       | Reviews and approves annual budget; monitors spending         |
| **Financial Reports**   | Receives quarterly financial statements                       |
| **Audit**               | Arranges annual independent audit of non-profit finances      |
| **Major Expenses**      | Approves any expense over threshold (e.g., €50k)              |
| **Compensation**        | Ensures salaries align with published scales                  |
| **Profit Distribution** | Verifies the 50/50 split between employee bonuses and OS pool |

#### 2. **Mission & Integrity Guidance**

The Board acts as the ethical guardian of the organization's mission.

| Responsibility            | Details                                                        |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Mission Alignment**     | Reviews that projects and decisions serve regenerative mission |
| **Ethical Audits**        | Receives and reviews annual ethics compliance audits           |
| **Conflict Management**   | Advises on resolving stakeholder conflicts                     |
| **Value Drift Detection** | Flags emerging issues that could compromise integrity          |
| **Accountability**        | Holds C-level accountable to mission commitments               |

#### 3. **Strategic Planning**

The Board partners with C-level leadership on long-term vision and strategy.

| Activity                | Details                                             |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **3-5 Year Vision**     | Co-creates strategic direction with C-suite         |
| **Annual Planning**     | Reviews annual goals and how they serve strategy    |
| **Capability Building** | Advises on developing new competencies or strengths |
| **Research Agenda**     | Supports think-tank research direction and coverage |
| **Market Positioning**  | Discusses how to communicate our unique value       |

#### 4. **Open Source Governance**

The Board reviews and approves how the 50% Open Source profit pool is allocated.

| Duty                  | Details                                                    |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Project Selection** | Reviews which OS projects receive funding support          |
| **Impact Assessment** | Measures whether funded projects advance cultural heritage |
| **Diversification**   | Ensures funding serves multiple communities and causes     |
| **Reporting**         | Publishes annual report on OS contributions and impact     |

#### 5. **Organizational Health**

The Board monitors organizational culture, employee wellbeing, and compensation equity.

| Area                      | Details                                                           |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Culture**               | Observes whether regenerative values are lived, not just espoused |
| **Retention**             | Reviews employee satisfaction, turnover, burnout indicators       |
| **Compensation Equity**   | Verifies salary scales are fair and transparent                   |
| **Diversity & Inclusion** | Assesses whether team reflects multiple perspectives              |
| **Board Diversity**       | Intentionally recruits diverse Board members                      |

#### 6. **Risk Management**

The Board identifies and mitigates organizational risks.

| Risk Category    | Mitigation                                         |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Financial**    | Maintains cash reserves, ensures funding diversity |
| **Reputational** | Monitors for mission drift or public criticism     |
| **Legal**        | Ensures non-profit compliance, reviews contracts   |
| **Capacity**     | Prevents over-committing beyond team capability    |
| **Burnout**      | Flags unsustainable work patterns early            |

### Board Meetings

**Regular Schedule:**

- **Monthly** — Strategic planning and issue handling
- **Quarterly** — Prepare for General Assembly meetings
- **Annual Retreat** — Deep strategic planning and Board development

**Open Governance:**

- Board agendas are published in advance
- Meeting minutes are published (with confidentiality protections for personnel matters)
- Employees and members can submit questions to the Board
- Annual Board report is presented to the GA

### Board Member Expectations

**Time Commitment:** Approximately 8–12 hours per month

- Monthly meetings (2–3 hours)
- Preparation and reading (3–4 hours)
- Project work or special initiatives (2–3 hours)

**Expertise & Learning:**

- Non-profit governance training (first year)
- Ongoing learning about regenerative business models
- Participation in annual Board retreat for strategic alignment

**Ethical Standards:**

- Complete annual conflict-of-interest disclosure
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information
- Represent the mission, not personal interests
- Recuse self from decisions where conflicts exist

### Board Succession & Development

**Regular Rotation:**

- 2-year terms with possibility of 1 renewal
- Roughly half the Board turns over each election cycle
- This ensures fresh perspectives while maintaining continuity

**Board Development:**

- Orientation program for new members
- Annual Board self-assessment and feedback
- Budget for Board professional development

### Accountability

The Board is accountable to:

**The General Assembly** — The GA can:

- Request the Board explain any decision
- Remove Board members who violate ethics or duties
- Amend Board charter or responsibilities

**The Public** — We publish:

- Annual Board reports
- Financial statements (publicly available)
- Research and impact findings
- Conflict-of-interest policies

---

**The Professional Board is our accountability mechanism for authenticity.** Its role is to ensure that the Lean C.R.O.W.D. doesn't gradually lose its regenerative purpose, even as we grow or face pressure.

A strong Board is how a non-profit stays true to its mission over the long term.
