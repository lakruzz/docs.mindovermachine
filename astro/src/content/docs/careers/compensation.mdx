## Compensation & Benefits

We believe in **radical transparency** about compensation. There are no secret salaries, negotiated rates, or hidden perks in the Lean C.R.O.W.D.

### Salary Structure

**All salaries are published.** Everyone at the same capability level earns within the same range.

| Level | Role       | Gross Salary Range (DKK/mo) | FTE Status          |
| ----- | ---------- | --------------------------- | ------------------- |
| 1     | Junior     | 14,000 – 18,000             | Part-time (student) |
| 2     | Associate  | 40,000 – 45,000             | Full-time           |
| 3     | Consultant | 50,000 – 58,000             | Full-time           |
| 4     | Senior     | 65,000 – 75,000             | Full-time           |
| 5     | Principal  | 80,000 – 90,000             | Full-time           |
| 6     | CxO        | 95,000 – 110,000            | Full-time           |

**Why ranges exist:**

- **Experience** — New to the level vs. deeply skilled
- **Specialization** — Scarce domains command higher range
- **Market reality** — External benchmarking ensures competitiveness
- **Individual contribution** — Exceptional impact within a level

**No negotiation:** Salary is determined by level and experience. We don't negotiate individual rates to avoid reproducing biases.

### The Profit-Sharing Model

The Lean C.R.O.W.D. reinvests 100% of after-expense profits according to this formula:

**50/50 Split:**

```txt
Annual Profit
├── 50% Employee Bonus Pool
└── 50% Open Source & Impact Pool
```

**Employee Bonus Pool:**

- Distributed to all employees (including part-time team members)
- Allocation is proportional to base salary level
- Typical annual bonus: 2–6 weeks additional salary (when profitable)
- Example: A Level 4 (Senior) earning 70,000 DKK gets proportionally more bonus than a Level 2

**Open Source & Impact Pool:**

- Allocated to board-approved open-source projects
- Supported projects might include: Linux kernel development, Debian, accessibility tools, regenerative research, etc.
- Annual report published describing all allocations

### Standard Benefits

**Health & Wellbeing:**

- Comprehensive health insurance (medical, dental, vision)
- Mental health and counseling services
- Gym membership or equivalent wellness budget
- Vaccination and preventive care covered

**Time Off:**

- **Minimum 25 vacation days** annually (per Danish standard)
- **Sick leave** — as needed, no penalty
- **Parental leave** — aligned with Danish statutory requirements
- **Sabbatical** — eligibility after 5 years (negotiable)

**Work-Life Integration:**

- Flexible work arrangement (remote, flexible hours)
- No unpaid overtime — work is 37.5 hours/week standard
- Mandatory vacation — burnout is tracked and addressed
- Career breaks supported without penalty

**Professional Development:**

- Budget for conference attendance (€2,000–€5,000/year depending on level)
- Training and certification support
- Book budget (€500/year)
- Learning leave (paid time for skill development)

**Technology & Equipment:**

- Laptop and equipment of choice (within reason)
- Home office setup budget (€1,500 one-time)
- Software and tools needed for work
- Internet stipend for remote workers

**Community:**

- Quarterly members gathering
- Annual off-site retreat
- Internal knowledge-sharing sessions
- Mentorship and peer learning

### Pension & Retirement

**Standardized Pension:**

- Employer contribution: 12% of gross salary
- Employee contribution: optional (tax-advantaged)
- Managed by Danish pension provider (e.g., PFA, Sampension)

### Leave & Time Off

**Vacation:** 25 days minimum (actual: typically 30 given Danish flex norms)

**Sick Leave:** Full pay, no limits (we trust responsible use)

**Parental Leave:**

- 14 weeks paid leave per parent
- Flexible arrangement (not required to be consecutive)
- Job protection guaranteed

**Flexible Work:**

- Remote-first culture
- Flexible hours (core hours 10:00–14:00)
- Sabbatical after 5 years possible

### Transparency Commitments

**What we publish:**

- Our salary ranges (you're reading them now)
- Annual non-profit financial report
- Bonus amounts and allocations
- OS project funding decisions

**What we review:**

- Annual market rate benchmarks (IDA, Prosa, industry surveys)
- Pay equity analysis (are there unexplained gender/demographic gaps?)
- Total compensation competitiveness

**Our commitment:** Salaries are living wages, not minimum subsistence. We pay to attract top talent, not to minimize cost.

### Why This Matters

**Traditional compensation:**

- Salaries negotiated individually (biases creep in)
- Bonuses tied to short-term metrics (perverse incentives)
- Executives paid multiples of staff (inequality)
- Benefits hidden or unequal

**Our approach:**

- Transparent ranges, no favoritism
- Bonuses tied to organizational health and regenerative impact
- CxO makes roughly 2–3x a Junior, not 10–20x
- Equal benefits for all
- Profits reinvested in commons, not extracted as dividends

This creates alignment. When everyone benefits from profit-sharing, everyone has incentive to make the organization successful. When salaries are transparent, people aren't secretly resentful.

### Special Circumstances

**Part-time work:** Available at any level if agreed. Compensation and benefits prorated.

**Contractor/freelance:** Available for specific projects. Rates aligned to salary levels (e.g., Level 4 contractor ≈ L4 employee annual / 1200).

**Sabbatical:** After 5 years, sabbatical opportunities available (3–12 months). Typically 50–75% of salary maintained, health insurance covered.

---

**Bottom Line:**

We pay fairly because it's the right thing to do. We share profits because they're generated collectively. We publish everything because transparency builds trust.

If you're considering joining us, you can review these salaries and decide if they work for you. No hidden numbers. No surprise. No negotiation needed.
