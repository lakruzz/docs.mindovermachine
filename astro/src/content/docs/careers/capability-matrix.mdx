## The Capability Matrix: Six Levels

We use a transparent, six-level capability matrix to define career progression in the Lean C.R.O.W.D. Advancement is based on mastery in three key areas: **Technical Excellence**, **Ethical Contribution**, and **Community Leadership**.

### Level 1: Junior (Student/Entry-Level)

**Gross Salary Range:** 14,000 – 18,000 DKK/mo (typically part-time)

**Role:** Learning and contributing under mentorship in a real, complex environment.

**Regenerative Excellence:**

- Demonstrates basic awareness of Green Algorithms and code optimization
- Identifies digital waste in small-scale test environments
- Follows established lean coding principles under supervision

**Systems Thinking:**

- Completes foundational training on the 14 principles of The Toyota Way
- Understands one-piece flow in the context of the team's pipeline
- Can explain the difference between linear and circular economies

**Participatory Design & UX:**

- Assists in documenting end-user feedback from participatory design sessions
- Can identify manipulative UX patterns in existing software
- Demonstrates basic accessibility awareness

**Open Source & Community:**

- Makes at least 5 documentation or bug-fix contributions to board-approved OS projects
- Attends at least 75% of internal knowledge-sharing sessions
- Participates in the Community of Practice

**Integrity:**

- Articulates the firm's mission of net positive contribution
- Completes all data protection training with 100% compliance
- Practices reflective journaling on ethical dilemmas

## How Advancement Works

**Annual Growth Review Process:**

1. **Self-Assessment** — Employee reflects on performance against level criteria
2. **Peer Feedback** — Colleagues provide input (360-degree review)
3. **Mentor Discussion** — Senior colleague discusses observations
4. **Leadership Meeting** — C-level reviews and decides on advancement
5. **GA Notification** — Advancement is celebrated and shared with community

**Timing:** Reviews happen once annually, typically at the end of Q4.

**Moving Between Levels:**

To advance to the next level, an employee must demonstrate:

- **Technical mastery** in the specific domain
- **Ethical consistency** in decisions and actions
- **Community contribution** through mentorship or stewardship

**No arbitrary time in level** — advancement is based on demonstrated capability, not tenure.

**Lateral Growth** — Employees can also grow within their current level (e.g., deeper expertise, new domains).

## The Matrix in Practice

The matrix isn't prescriptive. It's descriptive. It says: "Here's what mastery looks like at each level."

It creates:

- **Transparency** — everyone understands expectations
- **Equity** — objective criteria for advancement, not politics
- **Accountability** — progress is measured against clear standards
- **Growth** — everyone knows what development is needed
