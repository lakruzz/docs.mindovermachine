## Advancement Criteria

To advance to the next level, you must demonstrate mastery in three key areas:

1. **Technical & Regenerative Excellence**
2. **Ethical & Social Contribution**
3. **Community & Leadership**

This page describes what mastery looks like at the transitions between levels.

---

## Junior → Associate Transition

**Technical Excellence:**

- Independently implement green algorithms without supervision
- Understand energy optimization in a real project you've worked on
- Can explain code optimization tradeoffs (simplicity vs. efficiency)

**Ethical Contribution:**

- 100% compliance with data protection training
- Made at least 5 meaningful contributions to open-source projects
- Can articulate why regenerative practices matter (not just repeat them)

**Community:**

- Consistently attended community sessions (75%+ attendance)
- Helped mentor another junior colleague informally
- Participated respectfully in team decisions

---

## Associate → Consultant Transition

**Technical Excellence:**

- Independently led a small feature/project from design to delivery
- Demonstrated code quality that required minimal review
- Proactively identified technical debt and proposed solutions
- Showed proficiency with at least two key regenerative practices

**Ethical Contribution:**

- Led at least one internal ethics review or audit session
- Maintained "Reflective Journal" capturing ethical dilemmas and learnings
- Actively contributed to open-source projects (not just documentation)
- Demonstrated how a project served end-users (participatory design evidence)

**Community:**

- Mentored at least one L1 colleague toward advancement
- Participated in external FOSS communities (not just internal)
- Took initiative on team projects without being asked
- Showed commitment to transparent operations

---

## Consultant → Senior Transition

**Technical Excellence:**

- Successfully led a medium-sized client project
- Performed Lifecycle Analysis on a technical stack
- Showed anticipatory thinking — prevented problems before they occurred
- Demonstrated systems thinking across multiple components

**Ethical Contribution:**

- Ensured 100% compliance with complex data protection requirements for a major project
- Resolved a significant conflict-of-interest situation transparently
- Documented how client work served broader regenerative mission
- Mentored an Associate successfully toward advancement

**Community:**

- Managed multiple client-member relationships smoothly
- Contributed meaningfully to open-source governance (beyond just code)
- Spoke or presented on regenerative software practices
- Demonstrated care for team wellbeing and culture

---

## Senior → Principal Transition

**Technical Excellence:**

- Architected a large-scale system designed for long-term maintainability
- Established firm-wide standards that others adopt
- Demonstrated mastery of systemic thinking (not just optimization)
- Showed ability to mentor others in advanced design thinking

**Ethical Contribution:**

- Demonstrated "Transformation Mindset" — didn't compromise integrity for growth
- Managed organizational ethical gaps and pushed toward resolution
- Led Reflective Practice workshops sharing learnings with the team
- Advised on complex ethical dilemmas without clear answers

**Community:**

- Strategized Open Source contribution allocation (with Board)
- Represented the firm at external conferences or community events
- Successfully recruited or developed new talent to the firm
- Actively shaped the organization's culture and values

---

## Principal → CxO Transition

**Technical Excellence:**

- Established pioneering frameworks that advance the field
- Set firm-wide standards for regenerative engineering
- Demonstrated visionary thinking about software's role in society
- Mentored multiple Principals toward strategic thinking

**Ethical Contribution:**

- Maintained impeccable personal integrity even under pressure
- Served as primary ethical advisor to the organization
- Made difficult decisions that prioritized mission over profit
- Established accountability mechanisms that actually work

**Community:**

- Managed General Assembly representation and member engagement effectively
- Represented the firm's mission to external stakeholders credibly
- Fostered democratic governance processes that actually distribute power
- Demonstrated care for long-term organizational health over personal advancement

---

## Horizontal Advancement

You can also deepen expertise in your current level. For example, becoming a **Green Algorithms Expert** or **Participatory Design Master** while remaining a L3 or L4.

**Criteria for horizontal advancement:**

- Recognized expertise (inside and outside the firm)
- Actively teaching and mentoring others in your specialty
- Contributing to field advancement (publications, talks, tools)
- Maintaining current-level mastery while deepening one domain

---

## Advancement Process

**Timing:** Annual reviews happen December/January.

**Steps:**

1. **Self-Assessment** — You rate yourself against criteria
2. **Peer Review** — 3–5 colleagues provide feedback
3. **Mentor Input** — Your mentor shares observations
4. **Leadership Meeting** — C-level discusses and decides
5. **Feedback** — You receive clear feedback and a path forward

**Outcomes:**

- **Advancement** — Congratulations! New level, new salary range, new responsibilities
- **Recognition** — Honored for horizontal deepening or strong contributions at current level
- **Development Plan** — Clear skills to work on for next review
- **Conversation** — If there's misalignment, we talk honestly about fit

---

## What We DON'T Do

**We don't:**

- Advance people just because they've been here a long time
- Decide advancement behind closed doors
- Surprise people with a "no" at review time
- Expect people to figure out criteria on their own
- Make advancement about politics or favoritism

**We do:**

- Give clear feedback throughout the year
- Provide support to help you advance if you want to
- Make criteria transparent
- Celebrate advancement as organizational learning
- Support people who decide they're in the right place at the right level
