## Your Growth Path

Growth in the Lean C.R.O.W.D. happens in two ways: **Vertical advancement** (moving to the next level) and **Horizontal deepening** (becoming an expert in your current level).

### Vertical Advancement

**Moving from one level to the next:**

- **Timeline:** Advancement is reviewed annually, but there's no minimum time requirement
- **Criteria:** You must demonstrate mastery in three areas (see [Advancement Criteria](/careers/advancement/))
- **Process:** Self-assessment → Peer feedback → Mentor discussion → Leadership decision
- **Celebration:** Your advancement is celebrated with the team

**Typical trajectory:**

- Junior → Associate: 1–2 years
- Associate → Consultant: 2–3 years
- Consultant → Senior: 3–4 years
- Senior → Principal: 4–5+ years
- Principal → CxO: 5–10+ years

**No forced timeline.** Some people spend more time at a level deepening expertise. Others progress quickly. Both are valued.

### Horizontal Deepening

**Becoming an expert within your level:**

Rather than always moving "up," you might move "across" — developing deep expertise in a specific domain:

- **Green Algorithms Specialist** — Deep technical expertise in energy-efficient code
- **Participatory Design Expert** — Mastery of user-centered design methodologies
- **Socio-Technical Systems Architect** — Expert in organizational transformation
- **Open Source Strategist** — Leading major OS initiatives
- **DevX Champion** — Expert in developer experience optimization

**Deepening is equally valued to advancement.** Organizations need both "up and comers" and deep specialists.

### Learning & Development

**We invest in your growth:**

- **Quarterly learning budget:** €150–€600 depending on level
- **Conference attendance:** Travel and registration covered
- **Training programs:** Internal or external courses on regenerative principles
- **Mentorship:** Paired with someone 1–2 levels above you
- **Peer learning:** Regular internal knowledge-sharing sessions
- **Action learning:** Working on real projects with supervision

**Examples:**

- Level 2 wants to learn Lifecycle Analysis → sends to workshop, pairs with L3
- Level 4 wants to develop principal-level thinking → enrolls in systems thinking program
- L5 wants research skills → partners with think-tank team

### Mentorship

**You'll have mentors;** you'll also become one.

**As mentee:**

- Paired with someone 1–2 levels above early in your employment
- Monthly 1:1 check-ins to discuss growth and challenges
- Mentor helps you understand organizational dynamics and skillgaps

**As mentor:**

- L2 mentors L1 students
- L3+ mentors more junior staff
- Mentorship is part of the job, not an extra
- Time allocated for mentoring (approximately 5% of working hours)

### Performance & Feedback

**Annual Cycle:**

1. **Self-Assessment (October)** — You reflect on your performance against your level's criteria
2. **Peer Review (November)** — 3–5 colleagues provide confidential feedback
3. **Mentor Conversation (November)** — Your mentor provides their observations
4. **Leadership Review (December)** — C-level decides on advancement or recognition
5. **Feedback Conversation (January)** — You receive feedback and plan next year

**Ongoing Feedback:**

- Feedback is also continuous, not just annual
- Regular 1:1s with your manager (bi-weekly)
- Peer feedback is encouraged throughout the year
- Culture of direct, kind communication (versus surprise feedback)

### When Things Aren't Working

**If you're struggling:**

1. **Tell someone.** Your mentor, your manager, or a peer. We want to know.
2. **Get support.** We'll provide training, mentoring, or coaching to help you succeed.
3. **Adjust if needed.** Maybe this role or level isn't right. We'll explore alternatives.
4. **Never alone.** We don't believe in "survival of the fittest." We invest in people.

**If there's a cultural misfit:**

- Honest conversations about whether the Lean C.R.O.W.D. is the right place
- Help departing employees transition respectfully
- Maintain relationships (you might come back later, or refer others)

### Career Transitions

**Within the Lean C.R.O.W.D.:**

- Interested in shifting from client work to think-tank research? Possible.
- Want to move from individual contributor to management? Supported.
- Considering a change of specialization? Let's talk.

**External transitions:**

- If you decide to leave, we support a respectful transition
- References and recommendations from the organization
- You join our alumni network

### Your Role in Growth

**Here's what we ask of you:**

1. **Take responsibility for your growth** — Don't wait for someone to "develop" you
2. **Ask for what you need** — Mentoring, training, time to learn
3. **Share what you know** — Mentor others, publish insights, contribute to field guides
4. **Reflect on progress** — Honest self-assessment during reviews
5. **Stay aligned with the mission** — Growth is toward regenerative mastery, not just technical depth

---

**Growth is a journey, not a destination.** The Lean C.R.O.W.D. is structured to support your development across decades, not just years.
