## Our Open Source Funded Projects

The Lean C.R.O.W.D. allocates 50% of annual profits to support open-source projects that contribute to humanity's cultural heritage.

### Current Support Portfolio

We currently support and contribute to projects in these categories:

**Operating Systems & Infrastructure**

- Linux kernel development
- Debian distribution
- POSIX utilities and GNU tools

**Digital Accessibility**

- GNOME Accessibility initiatives
- Assistive technology tools
- Inclusive design frameworks

**Data & Research**

- Python scientific ecosystem
- Open-source data visualization
- Research publication tools

**Digital Sustainability**

- Green computing initiatives
- Energy monitoring software
- E-waste reduction tools

**Community Mapping**

- OpenStreetMap
- Location-based accessibility tools

### How We Allocate Funding

**Our process:**

1. **Research** — Identify projects meeting heritage criteria
2. **Staff Input** — Ask team which projects align with their expertise
3. **Board Review** — Projects are vetted for regenerative alignment
4. **Funding Decision** — Multi-year commitments are made
5. **Impact Review** — Annual reporting on project outcomes

### Nominating Projects

**We welcome suggestions** for projects to support.

**Consider nominating if:**

- The project serves essential public goods
- It has been valuable for 10+ years OR shows exceptional promise
- The leadership is welcoming to new contributors
- Funding would have meaningful impact
- It aligns with regenerative principles

**To nominate:** Contact our Open Source steward via [community contact page](/community/contact/).

### Annual Reporting

We publish annually which projects we funded and what impact resulted:

- Amount allocated
- Staff time contributed
- Improvements implemented
- Community feedback received

This transparency ensures our profit-sharing commitment is real, not just rhetoric.
